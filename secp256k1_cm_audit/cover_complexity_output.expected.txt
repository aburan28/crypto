---- Why Diem 2011 sub-exp does not apply ----

Diem 2011 ("On the discrete logarithm problem in elliptic
curves over non-prime fields and on hyperelliptic curves")
gives a sub-exponential DLP algorithm on Jac(C) for genus
g ≥ 3 hyperelliptic curves over F_q  *with q = p^k, k ≥ 3*.

For prime field F_p (i.e., k = 1), Diem's argument does not
apply: there is no proper subfield of F_p to descend to, and
the higher-rank factor base required for sub-exp construction
does not exist.

⇒ Over F_p, hyperelliptic DLP cost is polynomial in p with
  exponent strictly greater than 1/2 for every g ≥ 2.
  ECDLP exponent is 1/2.  Covers cannot help.

---- Could a cover over F_{p^k}, k ≥ 3 help? ----

A cover C/F_{p^k} → E/F_{p^k} gives DLP on Jac(C)(F_{p^k}),
not on E(F_p).  The ECDLP we want to break is on E(F_p), a
STRICT SUBGROUP of E(F_{p^k}).

Naive: solve DLP on Jac(C)(F_{p^k}) sub-exponentially (Diem
2011), then project back to E(F_p).  But:

  Solving DLP on Jac(C)(F_{p^k})  ≠  solving ECDLP on E(F_p)

The discrete log on E(F_{p^k}) is a different (and harder)
problem than on E(F_p).  Embedding E(F_p) ⊂ E(F_{p^k}) makes
the search space LARGER, not smaller.  No win.

This is the same fundamental obstruction that blocks GHS-
style Weil descent on prime-field curves.
