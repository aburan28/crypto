---- Hom_{F_p}(E, E^t) — slice-3 implication ----

For E ordinary over F_p, E ≁ E^t over F_p because #E = n
and #E^t = p + 1 + t = n_twist, and n ≠ n_twist (different
256-bit values: n − n_twist = 2t).
  n − n_twist = -864840773131319313704841732781346354654
  2t          = 864840773131319313704841732781346354654
  Equal? 1

Hence Hom_{F_p}(E, E^t) = 0 (no F_p-rational isogeny between
them).  Therefore End_{F_p}(E × E^t) = End(E) × End(E^t),
with no cross-terms.  The Néron-Severi group NS(E × E^t)_Q
has rank 2, and the principal polarisations are uniquely the
product polarisations (up to ±1 on each factor).

⇒ E × E^t over F_p is a *decomposable* abelian surface — no
smooth genus-2 curve C/F_p has Jac(C) ≅_{F_p} E × E^t.

This does NOT close slice-3.  The slice-3 hypothesis is that
some Jac(C) is F_p-ISOGENOUS to E × E^t (= Res(E_{F_p²})),
which is a strictly weaker condition than F_p-isomorphism.
By Tate, every F_p-isogeny class with Frobenius characteristic
polynomial (X² − tX + p)(X² + tX + p) contains E × E^t, and
MAY contain a Jacobian Jac(C).  The Howe-Maisner-Stein
criterion decides this combinatorially.

Concrete: a Jacobian in the isogeny class would arise as a
non-product abelian surface obtained from E × E^t by an
F_p-isogeny with a specific kernel structure.  The kernel
classes are in H¹(Gal, Aut(E × E^t)), and only a finite
subset corresponds to smooth Jacobians.  This is the proper
slice-3 search.
