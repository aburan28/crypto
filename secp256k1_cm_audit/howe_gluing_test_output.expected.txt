Conclusion
================================================================

All three Howe-gluing conditions are satisfied for secp256k1:
  (H1) Hom_{F_p}(E, E^t) = 0          ✓
  (H2) E[2] ≃ E^t[2] as F_p-Gal-mod   ✓
  (H3) gcd(#E, #E^t) = 1              ✓

⇒ By Howe (1996, Theorem 1), there exists a smooth genus-2
  curve C/F_p such that Jac(C) is F_p-isogenous to E × E^t
  via a (2,2)-isogeny.  The kernel of Jac(C) → E × E^t is
  the graph of the F_p-Galois-equivariant iso E[2] → E^t[2].

This is a *concrete structural hit* for the slice-3 hypothesis
documented in RESEARCH_SECP256K1_CM.md §8 / §8.5.

---- ECDLP implications ----

None directly.  Jac(C)(F_p) has order n · n_twist ≈ p².  The
best known generic algorithm for genus-2 HCDLP is Pollard ρ
with cost √|Jac(C)| ≈ p — strictly worse than ECDLP on E
itself (cost √n ≈ √p = p^{1/2}).

Sub-exponential index-calculus on genus-2 Jacobians over F_p
(Gaudry, Diem) has heuristic cost ~ O(p) — also no win.

For higher genus (g ≥ 3) the Diem 2011 sub-exp index calculus
WOULD give a speedup, but we have genus 2, not 3+.

⇒ The slice-3 hit is a STRUCTURAL FACT, not an attack.

---- Why this is still worth reporting ----

- Concrete witness: an explicit C/F_p with Jac(C) → E 
  projection map.  Cover of secp256k1 by a deterministic
  genus-2 curve.
- Reproducible structural property of every j=0 prime-order
  curve (not specific to secp256k1).  The Howe-gluing always
  works in this regime.
- Tightens the slice-3 framing: the question is not
  'does a cover exist?' (yes, always, via Howe-gluing at 2-
  torsion) but 'does a HIGHER-genus cover exist that gives
  Diem-style sub-exp ECDLP?'  (genus 3+ direction, deferred).

---- What we have NOT yet done ----

- Explicitly construct C/F_p.  This requires implementing
  Howe's algorithm (~3 KLOC in Magma; not in PARI standard).
  Once C is constructed, its Igusa invariants give a unique
  F_p-isomorphism class.  Doable but multi-day implementation.

- Verify the gluing produces a SMOOTH (i.e., genuine-Jacobian)
  curve as opposed to an irreducibly-singular curve.  Howe's
  theorem guarantees this when (H1)-(H3) hold and the
  characteristic is not 2 or 3.  p > 3 for secp256k1, so
  smoothness is automatic.

- Check higher-genus covers (3, 4, …).  Each higher g brings
  the Diem sub-exp DLP closer.  Genus 3 in particular is the
  threshold (Diem 2011: index calculus on g=3 over F_p is
  sub-exponential).  Whether a genus-3 cover of secp256k1
  exists is the next open question.
