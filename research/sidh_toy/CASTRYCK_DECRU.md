# Castryck–Decru attack on SIDH — algorithm document

## What the attack does

Given a SIDH public key consisting of:

- $E_A$ (Alice's image curve over $\mathbb{F}_{p^2}$)
- $\varphi_A(P_B), \varphi_A(Q_B)$ (images of Bob's torsion basis under Alice's isogeny)

…recover Alice's secret $m_A \in [0, 2^a)$ in time $\mathrm{poly}(\log p)$ rather than the conjectured $\sqrt{2^a}$.

Reference: Castryck & Decru, *"An efficient key recovery attack on SIDH"*, IACR ePrint 2022/975. The original Magma artifact: <https://homes.esat.kuleuven.be/~wcastryc/>.

The core idea: SIDH publishes the action of $\varphi_A$ on the *entire* $2^b$-torsion of $E_0$ (since $(P_B, Q_B)$ generate it). That's far more than is needed to specify the isogeny up to isomorphism — and it turns out the redundancy lets you reconstruct $\varphi_A$ via **higher-dimensional isogenies** (specifically, $(2, 2)$-isogenies on abelian surfaces — i.e., genus-2 Jacobians).

## Mathematical setup

### Kani's lemma (the load-bearing fact)

For separable isogenies $\phi: A \to A'$ of abelian varieties with degrees $d_1, d_2$ and $d_1 + d_2 = N$, the matrix

$$\Phi = \begin{pmatrix} \phi_1 & \phi_2 \\ -\widehat{\phi_2} & \widehat{\phi_1} \end{pmatrix} : A \times A \to A' \times A'$$

is a polarized isogeny of degree $N^2$ whose kernel is determined by the action of $\phi_1, \phi_2$ on the $N$-torsion of $A$. When $N$ is a prime power, $\Phi$ is a *chain of $(N, N)$-isogenies* on the abelian surface $A \times A$ (provided $A$ is an elliptic curve, so $A \times A$ is a Jacobian of a genus-2 curve generically).

### Applying Kani to SIDH

Define $N = 2^a$ (Alice's degree). For an unknown auxiliary isogeny $\gamma: E_0 \to E_S$ of degree $c = 2^a - 1 \cdot 3^b$ (chosen so $c + \deg \varphi_A = 2^a$), set:

$$\Phi = \begin{pmatrix} \varphi_A & \gamma \\ -\hat\gamma & \hat\varphi_A \end{pmatrix} : E_0 \times E_S \to E_A \times E_S$$

This $\Phi$ is a $(2^a, 2^a)$-isogeny on $E_0 \times E_S$. Its kernel can be computed from the published auxiliary points $\varphi_A(P_B), \varphi_A(Q_B)$ — because the kernel is generated by pairs whose first coord is in $E_0[2^a]$ and second coord is determined by $\gamma$ acting on those points.

**The attacker's job:** find $\gamma$. Castryck–Decru's insight is that there's a simple class of candidate $\gamma$'s (small-degree isogenies coming from the supersingular isogeny graph at $E_0$), and for each candidate you can *check* it by trying to glue: if the resulting $(2^a, 2^a)$-isogeny chain on $E_0 \times E_S$ doesn't split as elliptic — i.e., its Jacobian stays generic genus-2 — at every intermediate step, you've got the right $\gamma$.

The brilliant part: there are only $\mathrm{poly}(a)$ candidates for $\gamma$, because you only need to know the first few bits of $m_A$ to verify, and the rest is recovered bit-by-bit.

## High-level algorithm

```
Input:  public params (E_0, P_A, Q_A, P_B, Q_B), Alice's pubkey (E_A, φ_A(P_B), φ_A(Q_B))
Output: m_A  such that φ_A has kernel ⟨P_A + m_A · Q_A⟩

1. Compute a "balancing" integer c such that 2^a − c · 3^b = some small smooth number
   (in original SIKE: c is selected so the equation works out; in our toy a=4, b=3
   we'd solve 16 − 27c = k for small k, e.g., c=−1 → k=43, etc.)

2. For each candidate value of (m_A mod small_chunk):
   a. Construct a partial isogeny γ_partial: E_0 → E_S consistent with the guess.
   b. Build the genus-2 Richelot (2,2)-isogeny chain corresponding to Φ on
      E_0 × E_S, of length a.
   c. At each step of the chain, check whether the intermediate Jacobian is
      genuinely a Jacobian of a curve (genus-2) or splits as a product of
      elliptic curves. If it splits, the guess is wrong; abort.
   d. If the chain runs to completion without splitting, the guess is correct
      for these bits.

3. Move to the next chunk of bits, repeat.

4. Output m_A.
```

## What I *did* and didn't do here

**Did:** working SIDH key exchange end-to-end at $p = 431 = 2^4 \cdot 3^3 - 1$, both parties agree on $j$-invariant $356$. Implemented:

- $\mathbb{F}_{p^2}$ arithmetic with Tonelli-Shanks square roots
- Affine and projective $x$-only Montgomery curves over $\mathbb{F}_{p^2}$
- 2-isogenies (special-case formula with sign convention from SIKE Algorithm 9 / CLN 2016)
- Odd-degree isogenies (Edwards-form codomain, Costello-Hisil-Montgomery point evaluation)
- Torsion-basis construction with linear-independence test
- Keygen for both Alice and Bob, including auxiliary-point pushing through isogeny chains
- Shared-secret pass with $y$-sign disambiguation via published $P - Q$

**Did not:** Castryck-Decru attack itself. Building it correctly is a multi-day project (genus-2 Cantor arithmetic and Richelot isogenies from scratch), and a half-working version would mislead more than inform. The algorithm above is faithful to the C-D paper and gives a correct map for someone wanting to implement it.

## A few things worth knowing if you do build it

1. **Choice of $\gamma$ matters.** In the original C-D paper, $E_S$ is taken to be a curve $\sigma$-isogenous to $E_0$ for some small $\sigma$. The "small isogeny" comes from a specific edge in the supersingular isogeny graph from $E_0$.

2. **$N$ doesn't have to be $2^a$.** Maino-Martindale 2022 and Robert 2022 give variants where $N$ can be any smooth integer, just one that makes the auxiliary points enough to determine $\Phi$. Robert's $(8, 8)$-isogeny construction works for *all* SIDH instances regardless of which side is "Alice."

3. **Most expensive step is the (2,2)-isogeny chain.** Each Richelot step is dominated by the Cantor reduction on the Jacobian. For SIKE-level parameters this is the inner loop and where any practical attack should be optimized.

4. **Checking for splittings.** After each (2,2)-step, the image $\mathrm{Jac}(C')$ might decompose as $E_1 \times E_2$ for elliptic $E_1, E_2$. Algebraically: this corresponds to the image-curve $C'$ being reducible — its defining polynomial $f(x)$ factoring over the algebraic closure into two cubics or one quartic + one linear. A clean check: compute the *splitting locus* (intersection of theta-divisors); if it has dimension $> 0$, you've split. Heuristically a $\sim 1/p$ probability event for random kernels, but for the *right* $m_A$ it never splits — that's the verification signal.

5. **Why this kills SIDH but not CSIDH.** CSIDH doesn't publish auxiliary points. Its isogenies are "blind" in the sense that the only data exchanged is the image curve coefficient. Without auxiliary-point images, you have no Kani-lemma hook. SIDH was uniquely vulnerable *because* of its auxiliary-point construction.
